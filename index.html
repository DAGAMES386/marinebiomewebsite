All About The Marine Biome!

Location:

The marine biome is located all over the globe. It is in all the world's oceans. The marine biome covers over 70 percent of the Earth's surface.

Climate:

The climate of the marine biome has and average of 39Â° fahrenheit underwater with varying levels of sunlight. In shallower areas, there is more sunlight, and in the deeper ocean, it is colder and darker (see Sunlight Zones for more information). Many organisms depend on these differing conditions to thrive.

Organisms in the Marine Biome:

        Did you know that there are over a million animals and plants in the marine biome?

  Animals

        Some of the animals that live in the marine biome include dolphins, sharks, octopi, and tuna.

Octopus

        Dolphins are very interesting marine mammals. They have a sleek body shape that allows them to swim through the water at fast speeds. Dolphins have two pectoral or side fins, a dorsal or top fin, and a tail fin. The pectoral and dorsal fins help steer, while tail fin helps propel the animal forward. A dolphin's diet consists of fish such mackral, squid, and octopi. Dolphins hunt in pods which allows them to herd and catch the fish easier. Dolphins live in the marine biome because they are only suited to live in the water(though technically they have to breathe air) and there are no large schools of fish anywhere else.

        Sharks such as the Great White Shark have pectoral, dorsal, and a tail fin like dolphins, but instead of a horizontal tail fin, a shark's is vertical. Unlike dolphins, sharks are fish, not mammals. Sharks eat fish, dolphins, seals, birds, and sometimes other sharks. Sharks hunt their prey by ambush. Sharks live in the marine biome because it suits their food needs and the fact that they can only live in water.

        Octopi are molusks, not fish or mammals. They have soft bodies with eight tentacles and a small, hard beak for biting prey. They have suction cups on the bottom of their tentacles that allow them to hold their prey while they inject toxins through their beak that will stun or kill them. The diet of an octopus is crustaceans and small fish. The octopus has three special abilities. It can release ink when predators are near clouding their vision and allowing it to escape. Octopi also have a special pigment that allows them to change color and camoflague with the surrounding. This allows them to ambush prey and hide from predators. Lastly, since the octopuses body is soft and does not have bones in it, they can fit through any opening larger than their beak. This skill can be put to use when escaping from predators. Octopi fit in with the marine biome because they need moisture to survive and the marine biome provides it.

        Tuna are interesting fish with varying size, usually ranging between fifty centimeters to over four meters. As a result, their weight is also quite variable, with massess ranging from only just over one and a half kilograms, to over six-hundred and eighty. The diet of the common tuna has a diet of crustaceans, squid, and fish. An interesting fact about tuna is that they lack the ability to pump water over their gills. This means that they must keep swimming at all times to ensure they do not suffocate.

  Plants (and protists)

The marine biome is full with plants too such as seagrass, kelp, and algae.

        Seagrass is a plant that is found on the ocean floor in shallow bodies of water. It looks much like grass, but is entirely underwater. The length of seagrass ranges from around a centimeter to 7 meters! This plant is the food source of many marine herbaviors such as sea turtles and urchins among others.

        There are many varieties of kelp, but the main one (as depicted in the picture) has a foot like structure that holds on to the ocean floor and floats that pull the leaves and stem towad the surface. This stretches out the plant holding it up in stalks. The large leaves of this plant help it absorb lots of sunlight.

        Although it is techincally a protist, algae is usually considered a type of plant. Like plants, algae is a produces and uses photosynthesis to get it's food. Algae unlike plants do not have leaves or many prodominant traits that plants do. However, they provide a food source for crustations and small fish.

Sunlight Zones

        The ocean has three main sunlight zones: the euphotic or sunlit zone, the disphotic or twilight zone, and the aphotic or midnight zone.

        The sunlit zone is near to the surface and gets the most light of all the ocean zones. This zone exends to 600 feet below sea level. Plants like plankton thrive in this zone because of all the sunlight. There is so much life in this zone that 90% of marine life dwells there.


        The twilight zone has less sunlight than the sunlit zone and stretches from 600 feet all the way down to 3000 feet! Because of the little sunlight, no plants are to be found in this sunlight level. The only animals that are found at this level are accustomed to the dark, and use bioluminesence to produce light of their own.


        The midnight zone is the darkest of the sunlight zones - sometimes pitch black. This zone extends from the end of the twilight zone downward. Like in the twilight zone, there are no plants in the midnight zone. Since this biome is extremely deep, the water pressures are very high and it is very cold there. Animal life is only possible there because of the bacteria growing on the floor that they can use for food. This inhospitible sunlight zone is so big that it makes up 90% of the marine biome.
